# My Resume: 

[[Moksh Teng SWE Resume 10.pdf]]

---

🔹 **1. Be Impact-Driven, Not Task-Driven**

> ❌ “Worked on APIs”
> ✅ “Designed RESTful APIs in Spring Boot, reducing response time by 35% for high-traffic endpoints.”

🧠 Use the **Action + Context + Result** formula.

---

🔹 **2. Prioritize Relevance**

- Put the **most relevant experience up top** (backend, distributed systems, cloud).
- Trim generic coursework or outdated stack if space is tight.
- Use keywords from the JD (e.g., Kafka, Docker, Microservices, CI/CD).

---

🔹 **3. Metrics > Buzzwords**

- Showcase outcomes: latency reduced, traffic handled, revenue impact.
- Even if estimates, numbers make work tangible:*“Processed 1M+ messages/day via Kafka streams pipeline”*

---

🔹 **4. Highlight Ownership**

- Show where you **led**, optimized, scaled, or improved.
- FAANG-style resumes emphasize initiative:
*“Led migration of legacy monolith to microservices...”*

---

🔹 **5. Tech Stack in Action**

- Don’t just list tools. Tie them to use cases:
*“Used Docker + GitHub Actions to automate deployment pipeline”*

---

🔹 **6. ATS-Friendly Format**

- Stick to **PDF, single-column, no tables or icons**.
- Keep font professional: Inter, Calibri, Roboto.
- Bold only role titles, companies, and key results.

---

🔹 **7. Keep It Focused & Evolving**

- 1 page for <4 yrs of experience.
- Update every 2–3 months as you upskill or ship new projects.

---

---

🔗 Bonus Tip: Include direct links to GitHub, portfolio, or project writeups where possible!
