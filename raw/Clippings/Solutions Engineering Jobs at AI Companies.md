This is one of the roles I am willing to bet on most people sleep on. A software engineer thinks he is too technical to be a solutions engineer. A program manager or strategy consultant thinks they are very less technical and hence don’t fit the role. But here’s the thing, the solutions engineering is the single most important profession you have not considered as your career.

**What is Solutions Engineering?**

Solutions Engineer at someone who knows a product inside and out and is the first point of technical contact for the merchants to integrate that product. Solutions Engineering specializes in learning that product and understanding merchant use-cases and knowing how the product they work with is going to solve for those use-cases.

For example: at PayPal, we have a bunch of products like Braintree, Venmo, PayPal, Hyperwallet. My job is to understand merchant requirements and see how PayPal products can be a good fit. It also involves understanding merchant payments processing and what tech stack they use and how PayPal can be integrated.

**Flavors or Types of Solutions Engineering**

Solutions Engineering is a role with different flavors with varying levels of technical or soft skills required. I have given the role a rating from 1 to 10 with 1 being least technical and 10 being highly technical. Note this does vary from company to company. So, please do read the job description carefully!

Sales Engineer: Technical - 5, Soft Skills - 8

Solutions Engineer: Technical - 6 to 7, Soft Skills - 9

Integrations Engineer: Technical - 8 to 9, Soft Skills - 4

Solutions Architect - Technical - 7, Soft Skills - 8

Pre-sales engineer: Technical - 5, Soft Skills - 9

There’s other flavors to this role:

1. Deployment Strategist
2. Forward Deployed

So, if you want to be in this space, which companies would you target:

This is any SaaS (Software as a Service) company that has a complex technical product and is selling it to an enterprise. They require solutions engineers to:

1. Understand the product inside and out.
2. Know how the technical integrations work.
3. Get certifications in the product or even better build something
4. Connect or DM someone and share what you’ve built with them.
5. Interview prep.

Now, solutions roles depends on different domains:

1. Cloud - most popular and in demand. Think AWS, Google Cloud, Azure.
2. AI products - Open AI, Anthropic, Perplexity, Palantir all are looking for solutions architects.
3. Payments - PayPal, Stripe, Adyen, Cash App
4. ERP software like - Oracle, SAP

I am sharing some ways you can get or build projects for the above as well as certifications you can pursue.
