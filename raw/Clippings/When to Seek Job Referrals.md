Most people aren’t sure when to seek referral vs when to not. And this creates a huge issue. Because sometimes you just end up waiting for a referral and by the time you get it and apply, the position is already filled. So, how do you determine when to get a referral vs when not to. Sharing my strategy:

### Series A or very small company:

For company on Series A or very small company with seed investment, you target the founders, founding engineers, recruiters anyone working at the company. Your chances of getting and interview are very high if you do below:

1. Look up what the company is doing. See what problem it is trying to solve.
2. Now, use AI to find potential customers, see what features it should build and then use any vibecoding app to actually build a functionality.
3. Now, find thes hiring managers on LinkedIn and send them a very simple message.

Hello, My name is \[\]

I understand your time is valuable. I’ll only write three bullet points.

1. X years experience working for \[\]
2. Analyzed your company deeply and built potential features: \[share link\]
3. My education and experience aligns with role companies is hiring for.

Really love company’s mission and would love to intern/ work full time to build something awesome. Would love to chat!

Thanks,

\[Name\]

And if you are doubtful, this email works, this exact template was used by Niraj Pant to get a reply from Evan Spiegel, CEO of Snap and an internship at Snap!

### Unicorn Companies

These are companies that have matured beyond series C. Here again, you apply with referral but with a small change.

You don’t need to build something necessarily. Here you just need a person to advocate for you. So, if you have someone who can vouch and put in a good word for you, almost certainly, you will get an interview. Only thing is you need to have a position open.

1. Do this, once you find an open position,
2. find person working at the company on LinkedIn.
3. Send them a referral email or message simply sharing why you need to be hired.

Hello, My name is \[\]

I found a position at your company that really aligns with the education and experience

1. X years experience working for \[\]
2. I have experience in skillset of \[\] which are inline with role requirements.
3. My education and experience aligns with role companies is hiring for.

Really love company’s mission and would love to intern/ work full time to build something awesome. Would love to chat!

Thanks,

\[Name\]

## FAANG COMPANIES:

For FAANG companies, here’s the thing, again apply at the earliest especially if role is new. However, again seek a referral. And when you seek referral, again share your projects because unless you know someone personally, it will be a difficult to even get a referral. Here’s the email you share similar to one for series A- C companies:

Hello, My name is \[\]

I understand your time is valuable. I’ll only write three bullet points.

1. X years experience working for \[\]
2. Analyzed your company deeply and built potential features: \[share link\]
3. My education and experience aligns with role companies is hiring for.

Really love company’s mission and would love to intern/ work full time to build something awesome. Would love to chat!

Thanks,

\[Name\]
