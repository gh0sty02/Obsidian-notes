## SOFTWARE ENGINEER

---

**1\. Resume Optimization**

> “Act as a senior tech recruiter at a FAANG company with 10+ years of experience reviewing software engineering resumes. Review my resume below and do the following: (1) Identify the top 3 weaknesses that would cause a recruiter to skip it in under 10 seconds. (2) Rewrite my top 3 bullet points using the STAR format with specific metrics — include impact on system performance, scale (users/requests), and engineering outcomes. (3) Flag any vague language like ‘worked on’ or ‘helped with’ and replace with strong ownership verbs. (4) Suggest 5 ATS-optimized keywords I’m missing for a senior SWE role at a product-led company. Here is my resume: \[PASTE RESUME\]”

---

**2\. Job Targeting**

> “Act as a technical recruiter who specializes in placing software engineers at high-growth startups and tech companies. I am a \[LEVEL\] software engineer with \[X\] years of experience in \[TECH STACK — e.g., Python, Go, Kubernetes, AWS\]. I’m targeting roles at Series B–D companies and top-tier tech firms. Give me: (1) 10 specific companies actively hiring SWEs right now in \[CITY / REMOTE\], with a note on their tech stack and engineering culture. (2) 3 lesser-known but high-compensation companies I should consider. (3) The exact job titles I should be searching for beyond ‘Software Engineer’ — include Staff, Platform, and Infrastructure variants. (4) The top 3 job boards or communities where these roles are posted first.”

---

**3\. Cover Letter Magic**

> “Act as a professional cover letter writer who has helped engineers land roles at Google, Stripe, and Airbnb. Write a high-converting cover letter for a \[LEVEL\] software engineer applying to \[COMPANY\]. The letter should: (1) Open with a specific, compelling hook — not ‘I am excited to apply.’ (2) In paragraph 2, connect my experience in \[TECH STACK\] directly to a known engineering challenge at \[COMPANY\]. (3) In paragraph 3, quantify 2–3 achievements with real impact metrics. (4) Close with a confident, non-generic call to action. Keep it under 350 words. Tone: confident, technical, human — not corporate. Here is my background: \[PASTE BIO or RESUME SUMMARY\]”

**4\. Skill Gap Analysis**

> “Act as a staff software engineer and engineering hiring manager. I am targeting a \[LEVEL\] SWE role at \[COMPANY or COMPANY TYPE\]. Here is the job description: \[PASTE JD\]. Here are my current skills: \[PASTE SKILLS or RESUME\]. Perform a detailed gap analysis: (1) List every hard skill in the JD I currently lack, ranked by how critical it is to the role. (2) For each gap, recommend a specific resource to close it — a course, project type, or open-source contribution. (3) Identify any soft skills or working styles mentioned in the JD that I should reflect in my resume or interview answers. (4) Tell me which 20% of improvements would have 80% of the impact on my chances.”
